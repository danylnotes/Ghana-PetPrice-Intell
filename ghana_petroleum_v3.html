Acheampong, T. & Ackah, I. (2015)

Petroleum product prices in Ghana exhibit near-complete pass-through of international crude price changes. The study demonstrates that exchange rate depreciation amplifies price transmission, with every 1% cedi weakening delivering a 0.8–1.2% pump price increase. The deregulated pricing regime from 2005 removed administered price controls, making Ghana one of West Africa's most price-transparent petroleum markets — but exposing consumers to raw commodity volatility.

NPA & COMAC Policy Framework

The NPA Build-Up Methodology (Guidelines 26.03.2024) anchors ex-refinery prices to Platts Mediterranean CIF quotations at the official BOG interbank midpoint rate. The ESLA 2025 (Act 1141) transition from ad-valorem to fixed levy amounts was a deliberate counter-cyclical design: as IPP rises at high Brent prices, the fixed levy burden shrinks proportionally, constraining upside price exposure. The UPPF and PSRL act as the primary buffer instruments — though their fund balances constrain effectiveness in extended high-price episodes, as forecast for the Mar 16, 2026 window.
